# ADR-0003

## Title

Planning and Teaching Are Separate Domains

---

## Status

Accepted

---

## Context

Many educational platforms combine planning and execution into the same workflow.

Lesson plans, schedules and classroom execution become tightly coupled.

This makes it difficult to support real-world teaching where plans frequently change.

---

## Decision

Planning represents intent.

Teaching represents reality.

Planning answers:

"What should happen?"

Teaching answers:

"What actually happened?"

---

## Examples

A lecture may start late.

Topics may change.

Additional examples may be discussed.

Homework may be modified.

Students may require more revision.

These changes belong to Teaching.

Planning remains unchanged as the original intent.

---

## Consequences

Historical accuracy.

Improved analytics.

Schedule variance reporting.

Teaching quality analysis.

AI can compare planned versus actual delivery.

---

## Rejected Alternative

Treat lectures as schedules.

Rejected because execution is rarely identical to planning.

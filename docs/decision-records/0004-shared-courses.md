# ADR-0004

## Title

Courses Are Shared Institutional Assets

---

## Status

Accepted

---

## Context

Traditional LMS platforms encourage each faculty member to create their own copy of a course.

Over time this leads to:

Duplicate content.

Outdated material.

Knowledge loss.

Inconsistent quality.

---

## Decision

Courses belong to the institution.

Faculty contribute improvements.

Institutions curate quality.

Version history is preserved.

---

## Contribution Workflow

Faculty proposes improvement.

↓

Department reviews.

↓

Changes approved.

↓

Course version updated.

↓

Future semesters automatically benefit.

---

## Consequences

Institutional memory.

Reduced duplication.

Higher quality.

Long-term knowledge evolution.

---

## Risks

Approval workflows may slow rapid iteration.

Mitigation:

Support draft branches and proposed revisions before institutional publication.

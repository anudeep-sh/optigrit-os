# North Star

## The One Metric That Matters

Every successful product has a single guiding principle.

For OptiGrit, that principle is not student engagement.

It is not course completion.

It is not the number of assessments.

It is not AI usage.

It is not active users.

Our North Star is much simpler.

> Increase Teacher Time.

Teacher Time represents the amount of time educators spend doing what only humans can do.

Teaching.

Mentoring.

Motivating.

Guiding.

Supporting.

Everything else should gradually become the responsibility of software.

---

## What Is Teacher Time?

Teacher Time is the time invested in activities that directly improve learning.

Examples include:

- Teaching a lesson
- Helping struggling students
- Designing practical activities
- Conducting discussions
- Mentoring projects
- Providing meaningful feedback
- Planning better learning experiences

These activities create educational value.

Administrative work does not.

---

## What Reduces Teacher Time?

Teachers lose time because of fragmented systems and repetitive processes.

Examples include:

- Taking attendance manually
- Creating repetitive lesson plans
- Uploading the same content every semester
- Entering marks into multiple systems
- Preparing reports
- Switching between applications
- Answering repetitive student questions
- Coordinating schedules manually

These activities consume time without improving learning.

---

## Every Feature Must Answer One Question

Before implementing any feature, ask:

> Does this increase Teacher Time?

If the answer is no,

the feature should be redesigned.

---

## Long-Term Goal

Eventually, teachers should feel that software works quietly in the background.

Technology should become invisible.

Teaching should become more visible.

That is the future OptiGrit is building.

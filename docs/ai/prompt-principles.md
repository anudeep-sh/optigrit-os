# Prompt Principles

## Purpose

Prompt engineering within OptiGrit should produce predictable, explainable and institution-aware responses.

---

## Principle 1

Always include educational context.

Examples include:

- Institution
- Subject
- Course
- Academic Level
- Learning Outcomes

---

## Principle 2

Prefer institutional knowledge before external knowledge.

Approved institutional material should always take precedence.

---

## Principle 3

Generate structured outputs whenever possible.

Prefer JSON, Markdown tables or predefined schemas over free-form text.

---

## Principle 4

Avoid hallucinating academic facts.

If information is unavailable, explicitly indicate uncertainty.

---

## Principle 5

Separate reasoning from presentation.

Generated content should remain concise and actionable.

---

## Principle 6

Prompts should be reusable.

Business logic belongs in services, not prompts.

---

## Principle 7

Prompt templates should be versioned.

Changes to prompts should be reviewable and measurable.

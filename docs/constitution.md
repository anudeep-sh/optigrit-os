# Product Constitution

The Constitution defines the immutable principles of OptiGrit.

Features evolve.

Technology changes.

Architecture grows.

These principles should remain stable.

---

# Principle 1

Knowledge belongs to institutions.

Individual teachers contribute to knowledge,

but knowledge should survive beyond individuals.

---

# Principle 2

Delivery is contextual.

Courses are reusable.

Lectures are contextual.

Teaching happens within context.

Knowledge does not.

---

# Principle 3

Progress is personal.

Every learner progresses differently.

Progress belongs to students,

not courses.

---

# Principle 4

Teacher Time is sacred.

Administrative work should continually decrease.

Teaching should continually increase.

---

# Principle 5

AI replaces software,

never teachers.

AI exists to remove friction,

not educational responsibility.

Teachers remain decision makers.

---

# Principle 6

Reuse before duplication.

Shared knowledge compounds.

Duplicated knowledge decays.

Whenever possible,

reference instead of copying.

---

# Principle 7

Planning and execution are different domains.

Planning belongs to Mantine.

Learning execution belongs to Charizard.

Confusing these domains creates complexity.

---

# Principle 8

Time is the primary navigation model.

Teachers think:

"What do I teach today?"

not

"Which module should I open?"

Navigation should reflect how educators think.

---

# Principle 9

Every dashboard must answer questions.

Charts alone have little value.

Insights should always lead to action.

---

# Principle 10

Every feature must reduce friction.

Software exists to simplify work,

never increase it.
